XI. And, to prevent any Exadions and unreasonable Demands from the respective Tenants or Lessees

under the said Trustees, be it enacted, That the said Trustees shall, in every Agreement they shall enter

into for the leafing or letting such Wharfs, Docks, or other Works as aforesaid, expressly mention what

Sums shall be demanded or taken by the respective Tenants or Lessees, for the Use of such Wharfs,

Docks, or other Works, of the Master or Owner of any Ship or Vessel occupying the fame ; and if such

Tenant or Lessee shall demand or take more than the Sum stipulated by such Agreement, such Tenant or

Lessee shall, for every such Offence, forfeit Treble the Sum demanded more than he {hall be allowed to

take by such Agreement; to be recovered and levied in Manner herein after mentioned ; one Moiety of

which Forfeiture shall be applied to the enlarging or maintaining the said Port and Harbour, and the other

Moiety to such Person or Persons as shall prosecute, sue for, and recover the fame..

XII. And be it further enacted, That from and after the twenty-fourth Day of July, one thousand

seven hundred and sixty-seven, no Person or Persons whatsoever shall throw or empty any Ballast, Earth,

Dust, Arties, Rubbish, or Stones, into the said Port and Harbour, or take away any Ballast, Shingle,

Stones, or other Things, the Removal or taking away of which may be prejudicial to the said Port and

Harbour, or to the Works erected, or to be erected, for the Security of the fame, or do any Ad, Matter,

or Thing, to prejudice or annoy the fame : And if any such Prejudice or Annoyance shall be done, the

Matter thereof shall be examined into by the said Trustees hereby appointed, or by virtue of this Act to

be appointed, from time to time, or any two or more of them, who are hereby impowered, for every such

Offence, to impose any Fine or Fines upon every or any Person or Persons so offending, not exceeding

the Sum of forty Shillings, which the said Trustees, or any two or more of them, by Warrant under their

Hands and Seals, directed to such Person or Persons as the said Trustees, or any two or more of them,

shall, for that Purpose, name or appoint, are hereby impowered to levy and raise by Distress and Sale of

the respective Goods and Chattels of every or any such Offender or Offenders, rendering to him or them

the Overplus, if any there be, and he, (he, or they, shall demand the fame; and the said Fine or Fines,

when levied, shall be paid to such Person or Persons as the said Trustees, or any two or more of them,

shall direct and appoint to receive the same, to be applied as herein after directed.

XIII. And, for the more orderly Government of the said Port and Harbour, and for the better pre-

- serving the fame, and the Pier or Piers, and other Works, now erected, or hereafter to be erected, there,

from Prejudice or Damage, be it enacted, That the Trustees, or any five or more of them, shall have

Power and Authority, and they are hereby authorised and impowered, to nominate and appoint, by Wri-

ting under their Hands and Seals, a Person or Persons as they shall think proper, with such annual Allow-

ance or Salary out of the Rates and Duties by this Act granted and made payable as they shall think fit,

not exceeding ten Pounds per Annum ; and such Person or Persons so appointed shall, from time to time,

order and require all and every or any Person or Persons having the Command or Rule of any Ship, Bark,

or other Vessel, entering into, lying, being, or abiding, within the said Port or Harbour of Saint Ives, to

lie, anchor, moor, and ballast, the fame, in such proper Place or Places of the said Harbour, as the said

Person or Persons so appointed shall order, assign, or direct, for that Purpose ; provided that the Person or

Persons so appointed shall, to the best of his or their Skill and Knowledge, order or require such Ship,

Bark, or Vessel, to lie, anchor, moor, or ballast, in a Place of Safety : And in case any Person or Persons

then having the Rule or Command of any Ship, Bark, or other Vessel, shall neglect or refuse.so to do,

he and they, and each of them, for such Neglect or Refutal, shall forfeit and pay the Sum of five Pounds;

to be recovered and applied in Manner herein after directed,
