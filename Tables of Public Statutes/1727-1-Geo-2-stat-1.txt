Acts passed in 1727, 1 George 2 stat. 1 c.1 - c.5.
From Danby Pickering, Statutes At Large, vol 15.
---

1. For the better support of his Majesty's household, and of the honour and dignity of the crown of Great Britain.

2. To enable his Majesty to be governor of the South-Sea company.

3. For enabling his Majesty to settle a revenue for supporting the royal dignity of the Queen, in case she shall survive his Majesty.

4. For continuing the imprisonment of Robert Blackburn, and others, for the horrid conspiracy to assassinate the person of his late sacred majesty King William the Third, of glorious memory.

5. For making further provisions to enable persons possessed of offices at the demise of his late Majesty, to qualify themselves for the enjoyment of such offices, and for altering and explaining the acts of parliament therein mentioned, in relation to qualifying persons for continuing in offices; and for the continuance of the sheriffs of the county of Cornwall, and county palatine of Chester, and several other officers therein mentioned, after the demise of his late Majesty, his heirs and successors; and for continuing such laws as would expire at the end of this session of parliament.
