Public acts passed in 1673, 25 Charles 2 c.1 - c.10.
From Danby Pickering, Statutes At Large, vol 8.
---

1. For raising the sum of twelve hundred, thirty-eight thousand, seven hundred and fifty pounds, for supply of his Majesty's extraordinary occasions.

2. For preventing dangers which may happen from popish recusants.

2. For enabling his Majesty to make leases of his lands belonging to the duchy of Cornwall.

4. For repeal of a clause in a former act, to prohibit salesmen from selling fat cattle.

5. For the King's majesty's most gracious, general and free pardon.

6. For taking off aliens duty upon commodities of the growth, produce and manufacture of this nation.

7. For encouraging the Greenland and Eastland trades, and for the better securing the plantation trades.

8. For continuing a former act concerning coinage.

9. To enable the county palatine of Durham, to send knights and burgesses to serve in parliament.

10. For the reviving the judicature for the determination of differences touching houses burnt down and demolished by reason of the late fire which happened in London, and for rebuilding the Navy-Office.
